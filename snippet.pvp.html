PvP

The Warrior class has a very reactive playstyle. You act in the error of your opponent and your burst is mostly RNG based (crits, sword spec procs). Due to this, it is often improper for Warrior players to take ownership of successful 1v1 outcomes.

On the other hand, the rage mechanic is forgiving errors and your abilities are effective with minimal effort. This allows you to focus more on macro play (positioning, coordinated efforts, BG objectives) as opposed to micro play (outwitting your opponents in 1v1 situations)

Basics

  • Have proper keybinds (all skills, all consumables, all gear sets, all on-use items)
  • Learn to flank, stay ahead of flag carriers
  • Learn to peel
  • Remember and use your unique utility: Mortal Strike, Hamstring/PH, Intercept+Charge, Disarm, Taunt and RNG weapon procs for insta-gibâ„¢
  • Have various gear sets (offensive PvP set, defensive PvP set, tank/maximum survival set) and consumables ready
  • Play!

Classes

Warriors are generally suboptimal for (fair) duels. However, in real world PvP the situation can be in your favor (e.g. getting the opener/Charge, enemy having used powerful cooldowns already). The pointers below will also help in BGs, since lots of encounters there could be seen as a chain of unfair 1v1 or 1vX situations.

Weapons

In general, swords and sword spec is to be preferred, because it has the highest burst potential and can proc off Hamstring. Dark Edge compensates the lack of sword procs with a proc that shuts down enemy actions, avoidance and can only be broken by DS/Iceblock. Combined with the fact, that it is the 2nd best weapon (after MoM) regarding dmg/stats, Dark Edge is the BiS warrior PvP weapon. You should leave Ashbringer/MoM for your paladin/shaman buddies.

  • BiS tier: Dark Edge, Crul/r14/AQ40 axe + Sapph shield
  • Swords: r14/Claymore/Ashkandi, Gressil + Sapph shield. Honorable mentions: Ashbringer, Bonereaver's, Zin'rokh
  • Mace tier: MoM, Sulfuras, TuF

Honor System

With Phase 2, the PvP honor system will be activated. You compete within your own faction for honor/standings. The absolute amount of honor you earn each week is not important, but it determines your standing. For your ranking progress, only three variables matter:

  • Your current rank
  • Your standing
  • The total number of players PvPing (= getting at least 15 HK) on your faction
